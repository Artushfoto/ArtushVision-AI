**Title: I got tired of keywording subscriptions, so I built a Desktop AI Tagger with 100% Local AI (Ollama) & Getty ESP Optimizer**

Hey r/stockphotography,

Like many of you, I'm a photographer who shoots a lot of travel and wildlife, and I got completely fed up with paying monthly subscriptions to keyword my own images. I wanted a tool that respects my privacy, runs on my own hardware, and actually understands the pain points of a microstock workflow.

So, I built **ArtushVision AI**. I’ve just released **v1.10**, and I wanted to share it with the community because it finally hits the major milestones I was aiming for.

Here is what makes it different from standard web tools:

*   **100% Free & Private Local AI:** It hooks directly into Ollama. You can run vision and text models completely offline on your own GPU. Perfect for unreleased commercial shoots, personal archives, or just avoiding API costs altogether.
*   **Getty Images ESP Optimizer:** This is a massive time-saver. It maps your tags against a built-in Master Dictionary of 9,800+ Getty-approved terms, uses AI to solve homonyms (e.g., "crane" the bird vs. construction equipment), and safely stores optimized terms in a separate XMP so your original metadata remains untouched.
*   **Hybrid & Cloud Options:** If you prefer speed, you can use OpenRouter (Gemini, GPT-4o, Claude) and pay only raw API costs through a built-in dashboard with total transparency. 
*   **Real Hands-On Control:** Drag-and-drop keyword reordering (crucial for Adobe Stock's top 10 priority), smart batch appending that doesn't overwrite existing tags, and an integrated map for GPS verification.
*   **Smart FTP Suite:** Multi-threaded uploads with per-agency limits (no more `421 Too many connections` errors) and automated CSV generation.

**No Subscriptions:** I hate the SaaS model for essential tools. It's a $39.99 lifetime license for the commercial version.
